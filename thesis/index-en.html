Collaborative medication management in mental health cases

.

A participatory action-research project with the aim of evaluating the potential implementation of shared decision making as good psychiatric practice, ensuring informed consent of patients and exploring the state of development of more efficient treatment monitoring systems in Spain, with emphasis on the protection of human rights and a personalised care model capable of achieving full recovery.

History of Collaborative Medication Management

.

Collaborative medication management in mental health is an initiative developed in the early 1990s in Quebec by research teams in collaboration with civil society and especially with mental health service users and human rights activist groups. In this region of Canada collaborative medication management is considered best practice and has been promoted by the Ministry of Health and Social Services. In recent years it has received a boost with the transfer of this experience in Brazil under the framework of a partnership between more than fifteen universities globally, and the establishment of an international observatory.

The practice is based on an approach of empowering the participation of the person using psychotropic drugs, especially anti-psychotic medication. Its purpose is to enable users, generally at risk of exclusion, to have access to all the necessary information on medication and its alternatives.

The practice is based on an approach of empowering the participation of users of psychopharmaceuticals, especially anti-psychotic medication.

At the Spanish level, research on collaborative medication management enjoyed a first phase from 2017 to 2020, co-led by professors Angel Martínez-Hernáez (Universitat Rovira i Virgili) and Asun Pié Balaguer (Universitat Oberta de Catalunya), funded through the RecerCaixa programme. As a result of this initial work, a guide and a workbook were published, tools that seek to facilitate a space for reflection and dialogue on the role that psychiatric medication plays in people's lives, complementing the already existing guides in English and French developed by the Quebec team.

Since its inception, the project has involved not only the work of academic researchers, but also the participation of people with experience as users of mental health services, as well as family members, carers and health professionals. Initially, research in Spain focused on the study of the following factors:

  • Experiences in Canadian and Brazilian teams involved in collaborative medication management, through exchanges, shared events and a review of the published scientific literature.
  • The potential for implementation in two adult mental health centres integrated in the public mental health care network in Catalonia (CSMA Badalona and CSMA Nou Barris), analysing the existing obstacles in collaborative medication management, the co-production of shared solutions between users, professionals and carers, as well as ways of addressing self-care in the treatment processes.
  • The dissemination of the results in scientific circuits, but also in the field of users, professionals and relatives through some channels such as the Sociocultural Association Radio Nikosia, which will actively participate in this project.

The current study

Currently the action-research project is focusing on the study on the implementation of collaborative medication management in mental health throughout Spain, and is being co-directed in the context of Enric Garcia Torrents' doctoral thesis by professors Angel Martínez-Hernáez (Universitat Rovira i Virgili) and Berta Vall Castelló (Universitat Ramón Llull, Blanquerna). The actions listed below will be carried out until 2026:

.

Reviewing evidence

Review of the scientific literature and medical evidence on the effectiveness of collaborative medication management in mental health, and the feasibility of implementation in Spain.

Review of the scientific literature and medical evidence on the effectiveness of collaborative medication management in mental health, and the feasibility of implementation in Spain.

Consulting leaders

Trace networks of power and influence by identifying what percentage of medical leadership and institutions support collaborative medication management, and what sectors among user and patient organisations are demanding its implementation.

Explore incentives

.

Map and review the national context in terms of ethical, financial and professional incentives that are affecting research into, and implementation of, new clinical practices.

Present and teach

.

Expose and discuss the skills, techniques and tools needed for collaborative medication management through digital classrooms, in-person classes at medical centres, and research focus groups with patients and practitioners to explore opportunities and pitfalls for implementation, providing the necessary training and oversight.

Read more -->

Sharing tools

Promote the development and integration of decision support tools and technologies applied to collaborative medication management, ensuring the quality of these tools through new best practice protocols, ensuring their integration into the electronic health record and services following rigorous certification systems.

Monitoring implementation

Establish valid measures to assess the extent to which patients feel informed and involved in decisions about their treatment and recovery, collecting feedback to help clinicians and administrators monitor progress.
